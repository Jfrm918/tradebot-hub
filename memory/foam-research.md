## 1. ENVERGE SPRAY FOAM CHEMISTRY & PRODUCT LINES

### 1.1 Product Overview
**Manufacturer:** Enverge (formerly SES Foam LLC)  
**Core Product Types:**

#### A. Open Cell Products
- **EasySeal .5** – flagship open cell system
  - Nominal density: ~0.5 PCF (pounds per cubic foot)
  - R-value: ~3.5-4.0 per inch (varies with post-foam curing & application)
  - Cell structure: Open cell (air-permeable)
  - Blowing agent: Water-blown, low GWP (Global Warming Potential)
  - Fire rating: ASTM E-84 Class 1 (Class A)
  - CSI Master Spec: 072119

- **SucraSeal** – patent-pending resin blend open cell
  - Similar density and R-value to EasySeal
  - Proprietary polyol + polymeric MDI formulation
  - Water-blown, low GWP

#### B. Closed Cell Products
- **OnePass HFO** – 2 lb closed cell
  - Density: ~2.0 PCF
  - R-value: ~6.0-7.0 per inch (blowing agent dependent)
  - Blowing agent: HFO (hydrofluoroolefin) – low GWP replacement for HCFC
  - High yields: 20-30% higher than legacy 2 lb foams
  - Pass thickness: up to 4" per pass (vs traditional 2 passes for 2 lb)
  - Compliant: ASTM C-1029 Type II
  - Fire: Class A
  - CSI Master Spec: 072119

- **OnePass HFC** – legacy formulation (being phased to HFO)
  - Density: ~2.0 PCF
  - Similar specs to HFO version
  - Older blowing agent (HFC); being replaced

- **NexSeal 2.0** – 2 lb closed cell (legacy SES product, now Enverge)
  - Density: ~2.0 PCF
  - R-value: ~6.0 per inch
  - Compliant: ASTM C-1029 Type II, CAN/ULC S705.1-15
  - Service temperature range: **-40°F to 180°F (continuous), 220°F (intermittent)**
  - Dimensional stability: ≤0.3% change @ -20°C (ASTM D2126)
  - Proprietary resin + polymeric MDI
  - Blowing agent: Solstice LBA (Honeywell), low GWP

- **NexSeal LE** – newer closed cell, low expansion
  - Density: ~2.0 PCF
  - LE (Low Expansion) = reduced rise characteristics
  - Easier handling in tight cavities
  - EU and UK market focus (ThermoFoam distributor)

- **ProFill CC** – cavity fill pour foam (not spray)
  - Medium density rigid foam
  - 2-component liquid pour system
  - HFO + water co-blown
  - Used for cavity fills, not primary spray

- **ToughFoam** – high-density agricultural foam
  - 10 lb closed cell (denser than standard spray foams)
  - Designed for agricultural/uninhabited storage buildings
  - Class A fire rating
  - Heavy-duty impact and abrasion resistance

### 1.2 Application Temperature & Environmental Limits

#### Open Cell (EasySeal .5, SucraSeal)
**Critical Parameters:**
- **Substrate Temperature:** 50°F–100°F (manufacturer recommendation)
  - Below 50°F: foam rise is inhibited, poor adhesion, incomplete cure
  - Above 100°F: excessive rise, cell collapse, poor density control
  - **Best window:** 55°F–85°F substrate temperature
  
- **Ambient Air Temperature:** 40°F–95°F (application window)
  - Outside this range: requires special formulations or test sprays
  
- **Relative Humidity:** 0–85% RH
  - Water-blown foam is sensitive to humidity
  - High humidity (>85%) can cause over-expansion and shrinkage
  - Very low RH (<10%) can reduce cure speed
  - **Dew Point consideration:** If substrate dew point is too high (>65°F dew point), adhesion issues occur

- **Moisture on Substrate:** CRITICAL – even light condensation causes adhesion failure
  - Enverge requires dry, clean substrate
  - Moisture interferes with A/B ratio mixing and isocyanate reaction

#### Closed Cell (OnePass HFO, NexSeal 2.0, etc.)
**Critical Parameters:**
- **Substrate Temperature:** 50°F–100°F (slightly more forgiving than open cell)
  - Optimal: 60°F–90°F
  
- **Ambient Air Temperature:** 35°F–105°F (manufacturer data)
  - Closed cell is more temperature-tolerant than open cell
  - Below 35°F: poor rise, incomplete reaction
  - Above 105°F: may require heated tank or reduced pressure settings
  
- **Relative Humidity:** 0–90% RH (more tolerant than open cell)
  - Closed cell foams use HFO/HFC blowing agents (not water-sensitive like open cell)
  - However, substrate moisture is still critical for adhesion
  
- **NexSeal 2.0 Specific:** Service temp -40°F to 180°F (application is 35–105°F range)

### 1.3 Substrate & Surface Preparation
**Enverge Requirements (All Products):**
- Surface must be clean, dry, and free of:
  - Dust, dirt, mill scale
  - Paint, sealers, oils (unless primers are approved)
  - Moisture/condensation
  - Frost or rime
  
- **Primer Recommendations:** Enverge advises consulting manufacturer for specific substrates (metal, wood, concrete, etc.)
  - Some substrates require primer; others do not
  - Refer to Technical Data Sheets (TDS) for each product line

## 2. GRACO E-30 REACTOR SPECIFICATIONS & OPERATION

### 2.1 Equipment Overview
**Model:** Reactor 2 E-30 (and E-30 Elite variant with InSite™ fluid sensors)  
**Type:** Electric, heated, plural-component proportioning system  
**Approved Use:** Polyurethane foam & polyurea coatings  
**NOT approved:** Explosive atmospheres or hazardous (classified) locations

### 2.2 Specifications
**Heating & Pressure:**
- **Heater Type:** Electric, thermostatic control
- **Dual Pump Configuration:** Two positive-displacement pumps (A-side & B-side independent)
- **Proportioning:** 1:1 to custom ratios (electronically controlled)
- **Pressure Capability:** Typically 3000 PSI maximum (verified on nameplate)
  - **Recommended working pressure for daily use (8-10 hrs/day):** 1500–2000 PSI
  - Heavy cycle operators: stay at lower pressure to reduce pump wear
  
**Temperature Control:**
- **Tank Temperature Settings:** Typically 120°F–140°F (A-side and B-side independently adjustable)
  - Winter (cold material): higher temps (130–140°F)
  - Summer (warm material): lower temps (120–130°F)
  - Goal: consistent spray-gun outlet temperature (130–150°F typical)

**Fluid Inlet Sensors (Elite models):**
- Monitor fluid flow and detect:
  - Loss of feed pressure
  - Pump seal failure
  - Clogged inlet filter
  - Fluid leaks
- Status code 24: Pressure imbalance detection (off-ratio warning)

**Hose Systems:**
- **Standard:** 50 ft heated hose (Xtreme-Wrap)
- **300 ft hose runs:** Documented effect on heat retention, pressure drop, A/B ratio
  - Longer runs = greater heat loss from chemical
  - Pressure drop proportional to hose length and restriction
  - **Field experience:** 300 ft runs require higher reactor temps and careful pressure monitoring
  - **20 ft whip vs 10 ft whip:** 20 ft whips have measurable spray delay and temp loss; 10 ft preferred for responsive feedback

### 2.3 Daily Maintenance & Failure Points (Heavy Use)

**High-Cycle Failure Modes:**
1. **Pump Seals**
   - Primary failure point under continuous (8-10 hrs/day) operation
   - Symptoms: Oil weeping, pressure drops on one side, off-ratio spray
   - Prevention: Check oil level daily, use correct hydraulic fluid (Graco spec)
   - Interval: Seals typically 3-6 months with heavy use (vs 12+ months light use)

2. **Pressure Relief Valves**
   - Can drift or stick if tanks not flushed regularly
   - Causes intermittent over-pressure or under-pressure
   - Prevention: Daily shutdown flush, monthly deep clean

3. **Fluid Inlet Filters**
   - Rapid clogging with heavy use (dust, polyol particles)
   - Symptoms: Loss of feed pressure, status code alarm
   - Interval: Check weekly, replace monthly (or 50 hrs use)

4. **Check Valves & Proportioning Spool**
   - Polyol residue can gum up check valves if not cleaned
   - Manifold spool can stick, causing ratio drift
   - Prevention: Weekly circulate-only run (no spray), monthly full purge cycle

5. **Heater Elements**
   - Mineral buildup on elements reduces heating efficiency
   - Can fail suddenly if tanks not flushed
   - Prevention: Use distilled water for circulate cycles, flush monthly

**Maintenance Schedule for 8-10 hrs/day, 5-6 days/week:**
- **Daily:** Oil level check, pressure test (verify both sides within 50 PSI), temperature monitor
- **Weekly:** Circulate-only shutdown purge (no spray), visual inspection for leaks
- **Monthly:** Deep tank flush, filter replacement, heater inspection
- **Every 3 months:** Pump seal inspection, pressure relief valve check, full system diagnostic
- **Every 6 months:** Hose inspection (color, texture, cracks), consider seal rebuild if showing wear

### 2.4 Pressure & Temperature Calibration Best Practices

**Pressure Imbalance Setting (Status Code 24):**
- Default: typically 150–200 PSI difference triggers alarm
- For heavy-use operators: set to 100–150 PSI (tighter tolerance) to catch pump wear early
- **Do NOT disable** pressure imbalance checking; it's early warning for seals

**Temperature Probes:**
- Graco E-30 has *tank* temperature sensors, not outlet temperature sensors
- **Outlet temperature lags tank temperature** by 10–20°F depending on hose length and ambient
- **Best practice:** Use separate thermometer on hose outlet during dial-in
  - Spray gun outlet should be 130–150°F for Enverge foam
  - If outlet is 120°F, reactor tanks may need 140–150°F setting

**Pressure Settings for Enverge Foam:**
- Open cell (EasySeal): 1200–1800 PSI (varies by application, gun model)
- Closed cell (OnePass): 1500–2200 PSI (depends on density, viscosity)
- Dial-in on test card before job; adjust for:
  - Flow rate (speed of application)
  - Pattern width
  - Spray smoothness & cell structure

## 3. GRACO FUSION AP GUN & TIP SELECTION

### 3.1 Fusion AP Spray Gun Basics
**Type:** Air-Purge (AP) spray head for foam systems  
**Function:** Atomizes and proportions A & B components, creates spray pattern  
**Operating Pressure:** Typically 1500–2200 PSI (system dependent)

### 3.2 Tip Size Selection: 4242 vs 4747 vs 5252

**Graco Tip Numbering System:**
- Format: **XXYY** where XX = A-side orifice, YY = B-side orifice
- Larger numbers = larger orifices = higher flow rate

#### 4242 Tips
- **Orifice Size:** 0.042" × 0.042" (A-side × B-side)
- **Flow Rate:** ~0.42 GPM @ 1500 PSI (baseline)
- **Best For:**
  - Detail work, small cavities, thin walls
  - Residential applications (tight spray control)
  - Lower spray pressure systems
  - High-precision application
- **Spray Pattern:** Narrow, tight, good atomization
- **Limitations:** Slow coverage, not ideal for large open areas

#### 4747 Tips
- **Orifice Size:** 0.047" × 0.047"
- **Flow Rate:** ~0.52 GPM @ 1500 PSI (24% higher than 4242)
- **Best For:**
  - **MOST COMMON** for commercial spray foam work
  - Balanced detail + speed
  - Both open and closed cell foams
  - Retrofit work, mixed applications
- **Spray Pattern:** Medium width, good atomization, versatile
- **Performance:** Workhorse tip; reliable across temp/humidity ranges

#### 5252 Tips
- **Orifice Size:** 0.052" × 0.052"
- **Flow Rate:** ~0.62 GPM @ 1500 PSI (48% higher than 4242)
- **Best For:**
  - Large open areas (attics, crawlspaces, big cavities)
  - High-production commercial/industrial work
  - Systems with robust pressure capacity (2000+ PSI)
  - New construction (not retrofit where precision matters)
- **Spray Pattern:** Wide, higher volume, faster coverage
- **Limitations:** Less precision in tight spaces, can overspray; overkill for detail work

### 3.3 Performance in Hot/Cold/Humid Conditions

#### Hot Ambient (90°F–110°F+)
- **Material viscosity:** Lower (flows easier, less atomization resistance)
- **Pressure requirement:** May need 100–200 PSI higher to maintain pattern
- **Tip selection impact:**
  - 4242: Spray becomes overly fine, can drift; may under-apply
  - 4747: Maintains good pattern; recommended
  - 5252: Excellent for large areas; pattern stays wide and controllable
- **Recommendation:** Larger tips (5252, 4747) in high heat

#### Cold Ambient (35°F–50°F)
- **Material viscosity:** Higher (flows harder, more atomization resistance)
- **Pressure requirement:** May need 200–300 PSI lower to avoid over-pressure
- **Tip selection impact:**
  - 4242: Risk of overspray pattern, plugging if pressure too low
  - 4747: Sweet spot; can dial in middle range
  - 5252: May require excessive pressure; harder to dial for detail
- **Recommendation:** Smaller/medium tips (4242, 4747) in cold

#### High Humidity (>80% RH)
- **Dew point:** If substrate is below dew point, adhesion fails
- **Foam expansion:** Water-blown open cell can over-expand in very humid air
- **Tip selection:** Less critical than pressure/temperature tuning
  - 4747 remains default; adjust spray pattern via pressure dial
- **Critical action:** Monitor substrate dew point; do NOT spray if substrate is cooler than dew point

### 3.4 Graco Fusion AP Maintenance
- **Cleaning:** Daily purge with solvent (approved for foam systems)
- **Needle/Seat wear:** Visible wear → spray pattern degradation → replace tip cartridge
- **O-ring condition:** Dry-rot or compression set → leaks → replace
- **Spray pattern variance:** If pattern becomes asymmetrical or weak, swab interior passage with solvent or replace cartridge
- **Clogging:** Polyol residue can harden; avoid leaving pressurized between jobs

## 4. GRACO T1 PUMP

### 4.1 Specifications
**Type:** Positive-displacement piston pump (used in foam proportioning)  
**Configuration:** Often paired as dual pump set (A & B sides of Reactor)

### 4.2 Performance & Maintenance
- **Flow:** Varies by motor RPM and displacement (typically 0.3–1.0 GPM per pump)
- **Pressure Capability:** Up to 3000 PSI (system rated)
- **Heavy Use Issues:**
  - Piston seal wear: primary failure under 8-10 hrs/day use
  - Internal porting erosion if cavitation occurs
  - Thermal expansion: pump body heats; compensate with tank temp settings
- **Maintenance:**
  - Check case drain (low-pressure return) weekly
  - Monitor outlet pressure decay (indicates seal wear)
  - Oil analysis if available (early wear detection)
  - Preventive seal kit replacement: 6-12 months heavy use

## 5. OKLAHOMA/TULSA CLIMATE & SPRAY FOAM CHALLENGES

### 5.1 Summer Conditions (May–September)
**Typical Profile:**
- Temperature: 85°F–105°F+ (peak 100–110°F not uncommon)
- Humidity: 30–70% RH (highly variable; dew point 55–75°F)
- Solar radiation: Extreme; surfaces can exceed air temp by 20–30°F

**Spray Foam Impact:**
- **Open Cell (EasySeal):**
  - Substrate temps often at or above 95°F → over-expansion risk
  - Dew point swings (morning cool, afternoon hot) = adhesion risk during cool starts
  - **Mitigation:** Early morning spray only (before 10 AM), substrate shade management
  - High heat increases cure speed (beneficial) but reduces working time
  
- **Closed Cell (OnePass, NexSeal):**
  - More heat-tolerant than open cell
  - Blowing agent (HFO) behavior affected minimally by heat
  - **Pressure rise in tanks:** Hot material = higher pressure; may exceed 2500 PSI idle
  - **Mitigation:** Lower reactor temps, monitor system pressure closely, use larger tips (5252) to control pressure buildup

**Dew Point Challenges:**
- Morning dew point: 60–70°F (substrate cool, 55–65°F from overnight)
  - **Risk:** Substrate dew point threshold exceeded → condensation → adhesion failure
  - **Wait time:** Substrate must be >5°F above dew point before spray
  - Typical wait: 2–4 hours after sunrise in summer

- Afternoon dew point: May rise to 75°F+ as afternoon heats up
  - Humidity drops but absolute moisture increases (paradoxical)
  - Substrate temps 100°F+ → dew point margin safe
  - **Sweet spot:** 10 AM–3 PM typically safe in Tulsa summer

### 5.2 Winter Conditions (November–March)
**Typical Profile:**
- Temperature: 20°F–60°F (cold fronts can drop to 0°F overnight)
- Humidity: 40–80% RH (highly variable, cold air holds less absolute moisture)
- Dew point: 10–40°F (lower than summer)

**Spray Foam Impact:**
- **Open Cell (EasySeal):**
  - Below 50°F: rise inhibited, poor expansion, adhesion issues
  - Material viscosity: Very high; reactor temps need 140–150°F setting
  - Slow cure time: 24–48 hrs to tack, days to full cure
  - **Mitigation:** Insulated enclosures, heated substrate (propane heaters), schedule work during warmer part of day (noon–2 PM)

- **Closed Cell (OnePass, NexSeal):**
  - More cold-tolerant; specification says 35°F minimum
  - Curve ball: Closed cell has **higher dew point safety margin** because blowing agent is less water-sensitive
  - Material viscosity still high; same tank temp strategy needed
  - **Cure time:** Slower; may not achieve full strength until warmed

### 5.3 Seasonal Best Practices for Oklahoma Installers

**Spring (March–April):**
- Dew point unpredictable; check daily forecasts
- Wait-time post-sunrise varies 1–6 hours depending on overnight low and RH
- Both open and closed cell spray-able but monitor morning condensation risk

**Summer (May–September):**
- **Best window:** 6 AM–10 AM (early morning spray)
- Substrate management critical; shade if possible
- Closed cell preferred for production speed (more heat-tolerant)
- Open cell acceptable but risk of over-expansion; test spray on each job

**Fall (October–November):**
- Sweet spot for spray foam work
- Temps 60–80°F, dew point moderate, no extreme swings
- Both products perform predictably

**Winter (December–February):**
- Challenging; requires skilled execution
- Open cell: only during warmest part of day with enclosure/heating
- Closed cell: more forgiving; can work 10 AM–3 PM windows
- Expect 48+ hr cure times; job scheduling critical

### 5.4 Dew Point Thresholds for Adhesion & Yield
**Critical Rule:**
- **Substrate must be 5°F minimum above dew point** for safe spray
- Example: Dew point 65°F → substrate must be ≥70°F
- **Failure zone:** Substrate within 5°F of dew point → condensation risk → poor adhesion, bubbles, delamination

**Yield Impact:**
- Dew point-induced adhesion failure = not counted as complete coverage
- Rework required (removal + respray) = labor loss
- Open cell especially vulnerable; slight condensation compromises yield

**Field Practice:**
- Measure substrate temp with IR thermometer
- Compare to dew point (calculate from RH + temp, or use weather data)
- Wait until 5°F margin confirmed before spraying
- Re-check every 1–2 hours (dew point changes throughout day)

## 6. FIELD KNOWLEDGE: INSTALLER EXPERIENCE & TROUBLESHOOTING

### 6.1 Experienced Installer Tips for E-30 Reactor Dial-In

**Pre-Spray Checklist:**
1. **Purge System:** Run circulate-only for 5–10 min before first spray
   - Ensures consistent temp throughout hose
   - Clears any debris from overnight settling
   - Equalizes pressures on both sides

2. **Test Card Application:**
   - Spray 12–24 inches onto foam board or cardboard
   - Inspect for:
     - **Pattern symmetry** (should be oval, not skewed)
     - **Cell structure** (tight, uniform cells = correct ratio & pressure)
     - **Color** (should be uniform; swirls = off-ratio)
     - **Tack time** (should be seconds to 1–2 min, not hours)

3. **Pressure Adjustment:**
   - Higher pressure (within limits) → finer atomization, wider pattern
   - Lower pressure → tighter, narrower pattern
   - Target: 1600–1900 PSI for most Enverge + Fusion AP 4747 setups

4. **Temperature Adjustment:**
   - Tank temp too low → spray is stiff, pattern narrow, slow cure
   - Tank temp too high → over-atomization, misting, excessive rise
   - Target spray gun outlet: **140°F–145°F** for most conditions
   - Use IR thermometer on hose outlet, not tank gauge

**Ratio Tuning (Critical):**
- **Off-ratio spray symptoms:**
  - Sticky/gummy surface (excess A-side polyol)
  - Brittle, cracking foam (excess B-side isocyanate)
  - Swirled colors in foam (visible ratio imbalance)
- **Fix:** Adjust pump displacement or ratio dial on E-30
  - Graco E-30 has electronic ratio adjustment; DO NOT guess
  - Perform test card & measure final foam density (weigh sample vs volume)
  - Adjust in small increments (2–3% at a time)

### 6.2 Common Mistakes by New Installers (<2 years experience)

1. **Ignoring Dew Point & Substrate Moisture**
   - Spray during morning cool-down → adhesion failure on first coat
   - Visible symptom: bubbles, delamination within 24 hrs
   - **Cost:** 30–50% of job lost to rework

2. **Not Cleaning Tips Between Jobs**
   - Polyol hardens in tip → spray pattern degrades mid-job
   - Creates off-ratio zones (one side blocked, other flows)
   - **Fix:** Purge immediately after job, solvent-soak tips

3. **Running Reactor at Maximum Pressure Continuously**
   - Thinks "more pressure = better spray"
   - Actually: premature pump seal wear, cavitation damage, pattern deterioration
   - **Rule of thumb:** Use lowest pressure that maintains good pattern (usually 1400–1800 PSI)

4. **Trusting Tank Temperature Gauge for Outlet Temp**
   - Tank reads 135°F, but outlet is 115°F (10 ft hose in cold air)
   - Spray is sluggish, poor cell structure, slow cure
   - **Fix:** Use IR gun on hose outlet; adjust tanks 10–15°F hotter if needed

5. **Over-Relying on Visual Inspection of Foam**
   - Visible cell structure looks good, but density is off
   - Found out during post-cure test → job fails performance inspection
   - **Fix:** Weigh test sprays (3–5 samples), calculate density, confirm it matches spec

6. **Not Monitoring Hose Pressure Drop (300 ft Runs)**
   - Assumes pressure shown on reactor = spray gun pressure
   - At 300 ft, pressure drop can be 400–600 PSI
   - Spray gun gets 1000 PSI when reactor shows 1600 PSI
   - Pattern is terrible but installer blames equipment
   - **Fix:** Install pressure gauge at gun manifold, dial in based on actual gun pressure

### 6.3 Troubleshooting Bad Spray

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Check/Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----------|
| **Pattern is skewed/one-sided** | Off-ratio, clogged tip, pressure imbalance | Purge, inspect tip, check E-30 pressure difference |
| **Foam is sticky, wet, gummy** | Excess polyol (A-side imbalance) | Reduce A-side pump setting, test spray |
| **Foam is brittle, cracking** | Excess isocyanate (B-side imbalance) | Reduce B-side pump setting, test spray |
| **Foam is weak, poor cell structure** | Low pressure, low temp, or off-ratio | Increase pressure 100–200 PSI, check outlet temp |
| **Lots of fisheyes, small bubbles** | Contamination in tanks or poor atomization | Flush tanks, increase spray pressure 200+ PSI |
| **Foam shrinks 10%+** | Over-expansion, or moisture in substrate | Check dew point margin, dry substrate, lower pressure |
| **Poor adhesion, bubbles at edge** | Substrate dew point too high or surface wet | Wait for substrate to warm above dew point, clean surface |
| **Slow cure, tack time >5 min** | Low outlet temperature | Raise reactor tanks 10–20°F, check hose insulation |
| **Pattern is too wide, too much misting** | Pressure too high or tank temp too high | Lower pressure 100–200 PSI or reduce tank temps 5–10°F |
| **Slow application, narrow pattern** | Pressure too low or tank temp too low | Raise pressure 100–200 PSI or increase tank temps |

## 7. SPRAY POLYURETHANE FOAM ALLIANCE (SPFA) BEST PRACTICES

### 7.1 Core SPFA Publications & Standards
- **SPFA Technical Bulletins:** Cover application, storage, safety, climate
- **SPFA Technician Certification:** Industry standard; covers:
  - Chemistry fundamentals (polyol, isocyanate, blowing agents)
  - Equipment operation & maintenance
  - Climate & substrate considerations
  - Quality inspection & density testing
  - Safety & PPE (polyurethane chemistry hazards)

### 7.2 SPFA Recommended Application Conditions
- **Ambient Temperature:** 40°F–95°F (varies by product; check TDS)
- **Substrate Temperature:** 50°F–100°F (varies; open cell more restricted)
- **Relative Humidity:** 0–85% (product dependent; closed cell more tolerant)
- **Substrate Condition:** Clean, dry, free of oils, dust, moisture, frost
- **Dew Point Consideration:** Substrate must be ≥5°F above dew point

### 7.3 SPFA Quality Control Standards
**Density Testing (Most Important):**
- Cut foam sample 12"×12"×1" after tack
- Weigh sample (dry weight, post-foam)
- Calculate density: Weight (oz) / Volume (cubic inches) × 1728 = PCF
- **Tolerance:**
  - Open cell: ±0.1 PCF of nominal (EasySeal 0.5 = 0.4–0.6 PCF)
  - Closed cell: ±0.2 PCF of nominal (2 lb = 1.8–2.2 PCF)
- **Failure:** Outside tolerance → rework required

**Visual Inspection:**
- Cells should be uniform in size (tight packing)
- No fisheyes, craters, or voids
- No color swirls (uniform color = good ratio)
- No adhesion issues (bubbles, peeling edges)

**R-Value Verification:**
- Closed cell 2 lb: 6.0–7.0 per inch (product & blowing agent dependent)
- Open cell 0.5 lb: 3.5–4.0 per inch
- Full-thickness foam sampled; cores measured & calculated

### 7.4 SPFA Training Emphasis Areas
1. **Chemistry:** Polyol + isocyanate reaction rate; how blowing agent & water affect expansion
2. **Equipment:** Pump operation, proportioning, heating, pressure control
3. **Climate:** How temperature, humidity, dew point, and substrate condition affect application
4. **Safety:** Isocyanate exposure (PPE, ventilation, skin contact protocols)
5. **Quality:** Density testing, visual inspection, performance verification

## 8. MANUFACTURER GUIDANCE VS FIELD REALITY DISCREPANCIES

### Issue #1: Dew Point Margins
**Manufacturer Guidance:** "Substrate must be above dew point"  
**Field Reality:** Many installers spray at dew point threshold; some adhesion loss is expected and accepted  
**Impact:** Reduced yield (5–15% loss), rework jobs, adhesion complaints  
**Recommendation:** Stricter adherence to 5°F margin reduces callbacks

### Issue #2: Tank Temperature = Outlet Temperature
**Manufacturer Guidance:** Temperature gauge shows tank temperature; adjust for desired outlet  
**Field Reality:** Long hose runs (300 ft) cause 20–40°F drop; many installers don't account for this  
**Impact:** Spray is colder than operator thinks; pattern/cell structure suffers  
**Recommendation:** Install outlet temperature gauge; dial in based on actual spray gun temp

### Issue #3: Pressure Settings for Graco Equipment
**Manufacturer Guidance:** Varies by foam system & gun; refer to TDS  
**Field Reality:** Many installers run 2000+ PSI continuously (thinking "more is better")  
**Impact:** Faster pump seal wear, premature equipment failure  
**Recommendation:** Use minimum pressure for good pattern; target 1400–1900 PSI for most foam+gun combos

### Issue #4: High-Heat Ambient (100°F+)
**Manufacturer Guidance:** 95°F upper limit for most foams  
**Field Reality:** Oklahoma summers exceed 100°F; installers spray anyway with adaptation (early morning, reduced tank temps)  
**Impact:** Occasional over-expansion or slow cure; manageable with experience  
**Recommendation:** Acknowledge that 100°F+ is workable with tight control; not forbidden, just risky

### Issue #5: Closed Cell Specifications in High-Expansion Environments
**Manufacturer Guidance:** OnePass HFO produces 2.0 PCF at standard conditions  
**Field Reality:** In 105°F ambient, actual density can be 1.8–1.9 PCF (over-expansion)  
**Impact:** Lower R-value, potential structural concerns if spec calls for 2.0 lb minimum  
**Recommendation:** Test density in high-heat jobs; adjust pressure/temp to control expansion
