## The 5-Step Diagnostic Framework

When foam isn't looking perfect or behavior seems off during a spray day, Jason uses this 5-step validation. If all five pass, the job is acceptable and cosmetic appearance is secondary.

### Step 1: Paddy Test Spray
**What to check:**
- **Cream time:** Does it rise and cream within normal window (typically 3–5 seconds for open cell)?
- **Cure time:** Does it tack-free within expected timeframe?
- **Tackiness after 5 seconds:** Should be tacky but not wet; should hold shape if you touch it

**What this tells you:** If cream time, rise, and tack-up are normal, the chemical reaction is correct. A/B ratio is good. No off-ratio issues.

**Pass = ratio is dialed in.**

---

### Step 2: Cell Structure Adequacy
**What to check:**
- Visual inspection of cured foam: open cell should show consistent open cells, no collapsed structure
- No significant fisheyes, heavy shrinkage, or structural collapse
- Cell structure should match product spec for density/type

**What this tells you:** If cells are healthy and consistent, the substrate accepted the foam, pressure/temperature settings are appropriate, and cure conditions are right.

**Pass = substrate adhesion and cure conditions are correct.**

---

### Step 3: Foam to Depth
**What to check:**
- Confirm foam achieved specified depth (X inches as per job spec)
- No bare spots, no thin areas
- Coverage is full and uniform to specification

**What this tells you:** You're laying down adequate material, gun technique is producing proper volume, no missed areas that will undermine the job.

**Pass = coverage requirements met.**

---

### Step 4: Sealed Properly
**What to check:**
- Confirm foam adhered to substrate (metal, wood, concrete, rim joist, etc.)
- No lifting, no gaps between foam and substrate
- Sealed against air infiltration as intended

**What this tells you:** The foam is doing its job — air barrier is intact, no thermal bridges from gaps, job will perform as designed.

**Pass = functional purpose achieved.**

---

### Step 5: Installation Complete
**What to check:**
- Foam is cured and ready for next phase (trim, tape, finishing)
- No safety issues with uncured foam
- Job ready for customer approval or next trade

**What this tells you:** Timeline is on track, no delays or curing issues.

**Pass = project can proceed.**

## Operating Philosophy: Functional > Cosmetic

**If all 5 steps pass = job is acceptable. Don't chase cosmetic perfection.**

### Why This Matters

Most new spray foam installers obsess over appearance:
- Perfect pattern look
- Uniform color
- Pristine lay-down appearance
- Zero surface irregularities

This obsession leads to:
- Over-spraying (wasted material, higher cost)
- Excessive pressure on the spray gun (causes errors and inconsistency)
- Chasing impossible perfection instead of functional quality
- Slower spray speed (reduced production)
- Burnout (can't achieve perfection in real conditions)

### Real Quality = Functional Quality

What actually matters:
- ✅ Foam adheres properly to substrate
- ✅ Cell structure is sound and consistent
- ✅ Coverage meets specification
- ✅ Sealed against air infiltration
- ✅ Cured correctly with no off-gassing issues

Appearance matters **only if it affects function.**

Examples:
- **Chicken scratch pattern that cures correctly and adheres fully** = ACCEPTABLE
- **Perfect-looking pattern that doesn't seal the rim** = FAILURE
- **Slightly irregular surface that has 4" uniform depth** = ACCEPTABLE
- **Cosmetically perfect surface that's only 2" thick in spots** = FAILURE

## FoamDial Philosophy

This 5-step protocol becomes the **functional quality target** for FoamDial's algorithm. The goal is not to help installers chase cosmetic perfection — it's to help them dial in:

1. Proper ratio (paddy test)
2. Healthy cell structure (substrate + temperature conditions)
3. Adequate coverage (spray speed, gun control)
4. Air-tight seal (pressure, adhesion)
5. Proper cure (chemical conditions, time, environment)

Once these five are locked, the installer can spray faster, with confidence, knowing the functional quality is there regardless of how "pretty" it looks.

This is how FoamDial raises industry standards: **by teaching installers to optimize for function, not appearance.**

## Field Validation Checklist (Quick Version for Job Sites)

When something feels off:

- [ ] Paddy test passes? (cream time, tack-up, rise normal)
- [ ] Cell structure looks healthy? (no collapse, no fisheyes, consistent)
- [ ] Depth to spec? (full coverage, no thin spots)
- [ ] Sealed to substrate? (no gaps, good adhesion)
- [ ] Ready to proceed? (cured, safe, on schedule)

If all five ✓ = **you're good. Keep spraying.**

If any ✗ = **investigate and correct before continuing.**
